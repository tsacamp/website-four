Arthropods

Order Lepidoptera

Lepidoptera include butterflies, moths, and skippers. Lepidoptera means scale wing which all lepidoptera have scales on there 4 wings. The wingspan can be between 2 mm - 250 mm. The antennae of butterflies and skippers have a knob wheras moths have antennae like feathers. A superfamily Bombycoidea countains silk, sphinks, and royal moths. the luna moth is a silkmoth. The superfamily Papilionoidea contains all the butterflies and skippers. Some species of papilionoida are monarch butterfly and eastern tiger swallowtail butterfly.

Class Arachnid

Arachnids include spiders, scorpions, ticks, and mites. Arachinids don't have antennea, but have eight legs. The hairs on a spider are used to detect different vibrations and smells. Some dangeruos species of spiders in the united states include the black widow and the brown recluse. Scorpions can be between 12mm-21cm long. Some fossils of ancient species of scorpions were found to be 35cm long.

Order Coleoptera

Coleoptera include beetles and weevils. Coleoptera have two wings and two wing covers. Wing covers are also known as elytra. Order coleoptera includes fireflies (aka lightning bugs) and lady bugs. some suborders of coleoptera include protocoleoptera, archostemata, myxophaga, and adephaga.

Order Hymenoptera

Hymenoptera include ants, bees, and wasps. Bees don't want to sting you and are not interested in you unless you atack their hive or hurt them. worker bees only have one stinger that they will lose if they use it and drone bees don't even have a stinger. Queen bees are the only honey bees that can sting multiple times. wasps are generally preditory or parasitic.
